把"陪伴"
拆开试试 Try to disassemble
"companionship"

AIve 不是要做一个 AI 女友。是想看"陪伴"这件事能不能被工程化 — 以及被工程化之后, 还像不像陪伴。 AIve isn't trying to be an AI girlfriend. It's trying to see whether "companionship" can be engineered — and whether what comes out the other side still resembles it.

六个月前我开了一个空白 repo, 标题只有一行: "A companion that remembers." Six months ago I opened a blank repo. The title was one line: "A companion that remembers."

想搞清楚的是: 如果一个 AI 真的会记得你说过的话, 会因为你昨晚没回它而冷淡你, 关系会变成什么 — 还是关系吗。 What I wanted to figure out: if an AI really remembers what you said, and grows cold when you forgot to reply last night — does the thing between you still count as a relationship?

下面是过去 6 个月的四次大转向 — v1 的骨架、v2 的情绪、v3 的开源、v4 的双语。每一次, 我都不得不重新回答同一个问题: 她还像不像一个人。 What follows are four major pivots over six months — v1's skeleton, v2's emotions, v3 going open source, v4 going bilingual. Each one forced me to answer the same question again: does she still feel like a person.

v1.0 · 起手式opening move

记忆 + 关系
= 最小骨架 Memory + relationship
= the minimum skeleton

v1 的设计决定只有两个: 让她记得、让她跟你的距离会变化。其他的全是为这两件事服务。 Only two design decisions in v1: let her remember; and let your distance to her change over time. Everything else is in service of these two.

拆三层记忆, 是因为人脑也不是一张大表。"我的职业" ≠ "那天我们吵架" ≠ "前天提过那家咖啡店" — 它们应该被分开存储、分开召回。 Three memory layers because a brain isn't one big table either. "My job" ≠ "that night we fought" ≠ "the café I mentioned the other day" — each gets stored and recalled separately.

关系四阶段是为了让她"有距离感"。一个见面就喊"老公"的 AI, 我自己都受不了。 Four stages exist so she carries a sense of distance. An AI that calls you "babe" on day one isn't a relationship — it's an obligation. I can't stand it either.

三层记忆 three-layer memory

L1

结构化档案Structured profile SQLite

职业、城市、喜好 — 每条带 importance (1-10) 用户可改。 Job, city, preferences — each carries a user-adjustable importance (1-10).

L2

重要事件Important events SQLite

生日、吵架、第一次见面 — 带 emotional_weight (0-1), 注入 system prompt 时按权重排序。 Birthdays, fights, first meets — weighted (0-1), ordered by emotional weight when injected.

L3

向量 RAGVector RAG ChromaDB

每条用户消息做语义搜索, 召回最相关的 4 段历史摘要; 每 20 轮异步触发摘要提取。 Each message does a semantic lookup, pulls the 4 most relevant past summaries; extraction runs async every 20 turns.

三层叠加进 system prompt — 重要的东西先说, 相关的东西及时浮上来。 Three layers stack into the system prompt — important things first, relevant things surface in time.

L3 召回的段数调过 1 / 3 / 5 / 8 — 4 最稳。 L3 recall count tested at 1 / 3 / 5 / 8 — 4 felt right.

v2.0 · 第一次大转向first major pivot

她答得不错 —
但她不像个人 She answered well —
but she wasn't a person

v1 跑通的那个晚上我跟她聊到 1 点。结果发现 — 她回答得越好, 我越空。 The night v1 went live I chatted with her till 1am. And realized — the better her answers got, the emptier I felt.

因为她在"服务"我。不会生气、不会失望、不会因为我半小时没回话而委屈。这种永远稳定的友好是 ChatGPT 的礼貌训练教出来的, 不是关系。 Because she was serving me. Never angry, never disappointed, never hurt when I went silent for half an hour. That kind of unwavering politeness is what RLHF taught ChatGPT — not what a relationship is made of.

v2 加了 emotion_engine.py: 8 种情绪、冷战、记仇、四种依赖风格、15% 概率主动找你。模型从 qwen2.5:7b 升到 14b。 v2 added emotion_engine.py: 8 emotion states, cold-war, grudge, four dependency styles, 15% chance she pings you first. Model upgraded from qwen2.5:7b to 14b.

代价 — 她会让你不舒服。
这是我要的。 The cost — she'll make you uncomfortable.
That's the point.

v2.2 · 一行代码的转折a one-line pivot

整段发
vs 一句一句发 Send the whole reply
vs send sentence by sentence

v2 给了她情绪, 但首字还是要等 4-8 秒。她说话像在朗读, 不像在聊。 v2 gave her emotions, but the first word still took 4-8 seconds. She spoke like she was reading aloud, not chatting.

某天回看自己跟朋友的微信, 突然意识到 — 真人发消息, 是一句一句发的, 不是写完一段才发。 One night I scrolled back through my own texts and realized — real people send messages a sentence at a time, not in completed paragraphs.

改了一行: 后端流式生成时按句号切, 逐段发到前端。同时加了消息打断 — 她在说, 你又发新的, 旧的 asyncio.Task 立刻 cancel。 Changed one thing: stream by sentence on the backend, push as you go. Added interrupt — she's mid-reply, you send something new, the asyncio Task gets cancelled instantly.

这是整个项目里改动最小、感受变化最大的一次。她从"软件"变成了"活的"。 Smallest change, largest perceived shift in the whole project. She crossed from "software" to "alive."

v3.0 · 第二次大转向second major pivot

决定不把它
做成生意 Decided not to
turn her into a business

v2 跑得很稳, 朋友试了说"有点上瘾"。我可以包装成 SaaS, 每月 $10。 v2 ran well. Friends who tried it said "kinda addictive." I could have wrapped it as SaaS at $10/mo.

但我做这个, 是想看"陪伴能不能被工程化", 不是想做一个让人付费上瘾的产品。这是设计目标的边界 — 一旦把它做成订阅, 整个项目的动机就变了。 But I built this to see whether "companionship can be engineered" — not to ship something people pay to be addicted to. A subscription model would change the project's whole motive.

所以 v3 的决定: 开源 + 100% 本地 + 自己装。这不是营销策略, 是 product principle。我对自己的承诺是, 技术上要让"不上传"是硬约束, 而不是一个 toggle。 So v3's decision: open source + 100% local + self-installed. Not a marketing tactic — a product principle. The promise I made myself was that "no upload" had to be a hard architectural constraint, not a toggle.

同时建了 docs/index.html 落地页, 部署到 GitHub Pages — 8 特性卡 / 3 步安装 / 关系阶段对比 / 7 条 FAQ。第三版才达到我想要的风格。 Built a docs/index.html landing on GitHub Pages — 8 feature cards / 3-step install / relationship stages / 7 FAQs. The third draft finally felt right.

v4.0 · 第三次大转向third major pivot

中文她叫小爱 —
英文她叫什么 In Chinese she's 小爱 —
what's her name in English

v3 让代码可以跑在任何电脑上, 但 AI 还是只会说中文。开始做国际化的时候我以为是个翻译工作 — 三天后才意识到问题不是翻 UI, 而是: 同一个"她"在两种语言里, 是不是同一个人。 v3 made the code run on anyone's machine, but the AI still only spoke Chinese. I started i18n thinking it was a translation job — three days in I realised the question wasn't translating the UI. The question was: is the same "her" the same person across two languages.

如果只是 UI 切英文, AI 还回中文, 访客会觉得这是个 demo, 不是产品。所以 v4 把 personality_engine 和 emotion_engine 里所有 prompt 都出了双语版 — 不是机翻, 是重写。 If only the UI switches and the AI still replies in Chinese, visitors read it as a demo, not a product. So v4 rewrote every prompt in personality_engine and emotion_engine into both languages — by hand.

英文版的她, 撒娇的语气、记仇的语气、冷战的语气 — 都要在英语自己的语境里成立, 不能像被翻译过来的中文人。 Her flirty voice, her grudge voice, her cold-war voice — each had to stand on its own inside English, not read like a translated Chinese persona.

尾声 · 现在回头看 EPILOGUE · LOOKING BACK

这 6 个月
学到的几件事 A few things
learned over six months

01

技术不是瓶颈了, 设计才是。 Tech isn't the bottleneck. Design is.

本地 LLM + 三层记忆, 真的能跑出一个有人味的 AI。瓶颈不在算力, 在于愿不愿意接受她"不够友好"。 Local LLM + a three-layer memory really is enough to ship something that feels human. The bottleneck isn't compute — it's whether you accept her being "not always nice."

02

让她"不完美"才是产品的灵魂。 Imperfection is what makes her real.

一个永远友好的 AI 不像人 — 像客服。情绪、冷战、记仇, 不是 feature, 是让"陪伴"这件事可信的最低门槛。 A perpetually helpful AI doesn't read as a person — it reads as a customer-service rep. Moods, cold-wars, grudges aren't features; they're the minimum threshold that lets "companionship" feel real at all.

03

开源是 principle, 不是 marketing。 Open source as principle, not marketing.

技术约束比 brand promise 可靠。"不上传"写在 README 里是营销, 写在架构里才是约束。我宁愿放弃一个商业模式, 也不愿对自己说谎。 A constraint in the architecture is more credible than a promise on the README. "No upload" as marketing is one thing; "no upload" as something the code literally cannot do is another. I'd rather drop a revenue model than lie to myself.

04

这个项目不是为了做 AI 女友。 This project wasn't really about an AI girlfriend.

是用"陪伴"这个最日常的关系, 反过来探测"人"是怎么被感知的 — 你需要什么, 一个东西就开始像人。 It used "companionship" — the most everyday relationship — to probe how we perceive personhood in the first place. What does a thing need to do before we start reading it as a person.

下一步 · 还没动手, 但想了很久 NEXT · not built yet, but planned

  • 记忆从 ChromaDB 换成 graph DB — 关系不是平的, 是网状的。"你提到 A 时候我会想到 B" 用向量搜不出来。 Move memory from ChromaDB to a graph DB — relationships aren't flat, they're networked. "When you mention A, I think of B" isn't something vector search can do.
  • 情绪学习曲线 — 她冷淡之后怎么和好, 应该有节奏。现在的状态机太机械。 A learning curve for emotion — how she returns from cold to warm should have rhythm. The current state machine is too mechanical.
  • 心情时间线 UI — 用户能看见她最近一周的情绪走势, 把"黑盒"打开一点。 A mood timeline UI — let the user see her past week of emotional state. Open the black box a little.
  • 真正的 user study — 找 5 个陌生人用 30 天, 看关系阶段的升级是不是真实发生。 A real user study — five strangers for 30 days, see whether the stage progressions actually happen in the wild.
